Key Finding

Damien is a CYP2D6 Poor Metabolizer (*4/*4) with ZERO enzyme activity. This means CYP2D6-substrate antipsychotics (risperidone, aripiprazole) were effectively overdosed at standard doses. Additionally, he has CYP2C19 Intermediate Metabolizer and CYP2C9 Intermediate Metabolizer status, creating a triple metabolizer burden that reduces backup metabolic pathways.

Triple Metabolizer Burden — Compounding Effect

When CYP2D6 fails (PM), the body routes drugs through backup CYP2C19 and CYP2C9 pathways. But these are ALSO impaired (Intermediate), meaning drugs have fewer escape routes for clearance. This compounding effect is especially dangerous for drugs like citalopram (CYP2C19 primary + CYP2D6 secondary = double impairment) and venlafaxine (CYP2D6 primary + CYP2C19 secondary = double impairment — which explains the manic episode).
